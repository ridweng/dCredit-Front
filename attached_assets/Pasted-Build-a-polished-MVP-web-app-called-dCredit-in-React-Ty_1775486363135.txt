Build a polished MVP web app called dCredit in React + TypeScript, designed to run well in Replit and structured so the business logic can later be reused in React Native with minimal changes.

The app must be bilingual from the start, supporting:
- English
- Spanish

Language switching must be built into the product, not added later. All user-facing strings must come from a translation layer, with clean support for future languages. Default language should be English, with an easy toggle for Español.

High-level product:
dCredit helps salaried professionals with multiple debt products understand their real monthly debt capacity, prioritize repayments, and avoid cash-flow stress.

Main user question:
“How much additional debt can I safely take on right now?”

Target user:
Young salaried professionals, roughly ages 25–40, with stable monthly income, 2–4 debt products, and finances spread across multiple institutions.

Core MVP logic:
Use a transparent rules-based recommendation engine, not AI.

The app should estimate:
- Monthly net income
- Essential recurring expenses
- Existing minimum debt obligations
- Monthly free cash flow
- Debt burden ratio
- Cash-flow stability trend
- Safe additional monthly debt payment
- Approximate safe additional borrowing amount
- Debt repayment priority

Keep formulas simple, believable, and explainable.

Example rules:
- freeCashFlow = income - essentialExpenses - currentDebtObligations
- if freeCashFlow after a simulated new payment drops below a safety threshold, mark as Caution or Risky
- if debt burden exceeds threshold, reduce safe borrowing estimate
- if one debt has high APR and manageable balance, prioritize it
- if free cash flow worsens over recent months, show Tightening status

Every recommendation must include explanation text such as:
- “We estimate this is safe because your recurring income remains well above your fixed obligations.”
- “This looks risky because the new payment would reduce your monthly buffer below a safe threshold.”
- “This debt is prioritized because it has a higher cost and is putting more pressure on your monthly cash flow.”
